Reference

Mental shortcuts — think in structures, not in sums

Fast players don’t add faster — they recognize pot shapes. A single-raised heads-up pot, a 3-bet pot with a dead cold-call, a pot-limit raise: each has a fixed formula. Learn the formula once, then the table math is one multiplication.

Skill 1 · Pot tracking

Pots are built from blocks

HU single-raised pot = 2R + dead blinds
  • Raise to R, one caller: pot = 2R plus whatever the folded blinds left behind. Open to 3bb, BB calls → 6.5bb (SB’s 0.5 is dead).
  • Folded money never leaves. Blinds, antes, and abandoned cold-calls all stay in. A cold-caller who folds to a 3-bet leaves a full R behind — the classic miscount.
  • 3-bet pot, HU: pot = 2 × (3-bet size) + dead money. Recount once when the flop comes, then track deltas.
  • Re-anchor each street: memorize the pot going to the flop as one number; from there it’s pot + bet + call.

Skill 4 · Bet vs pot

Anchor on half pot

half pot first → adjust from there
  • Compute ½ pot instantly (halving is easy). Then: ⅓ ≈ two-thirds of that, ¾ = half + half-of-half, full = double it.
  • Reading a bet: compare it to your half-pot anchor. Bet 700 into 2,000 → half is 1,000 → it’s a bit over ⅓.
  • Pot-limit max raise (PLO, big-bet 2-7): raise to = pot + 3 × bet. “Call, then bet the new pot.”
  • First to act in a pot-limit game, your max bet is simply the pot.

Skill 2 · Stack in chips

Subtract as you act

behind = start − everything you put in
  • Update your stack the moment chips leave it, not at showdown. One subtraction per action is easy; reconstructing four actions later is not.
  • When you call a raise, you only add the difference — if you opened 300 and call a 3-bet to 900, that call costs 600 more, not 900.
  • At the end of each street, glance once: “I started 12,000, I’m at 9,500.” That habit is the whole skill.

Skill 3 · Stack in big blinds

Divide by the big blind — fast

bb = stack ÷ BB  ·  drop zeros first
  • At 100/200: cut two zeros, halve. 13,400 → 134 → 67 bb.
  • At 300/600: cut two zeros, divide by 6. 27,000 → 270 ÷ 6 = 45 bb.
  • At 200/400: cut two zeros, divide by 4 (halve twice). 18,000 → 180 → 90 → 45 bb.
  • Re-convert at every blind change — in mixed games the limits rotate constantly and your bb depth silently jumps.
